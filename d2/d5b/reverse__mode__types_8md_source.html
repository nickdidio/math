3 ### Array of Structures vs. Structure of Array types (AOS vs. SOA, matvar vs. varmat)
4 
5 There are two types of reverse mode autodiff in Math. They are colloquially
6 referred to as matvar and varmat, though it is more accurate to
7 refer to them as Array of Structure (AOS) and Structure of Array (SOA)
8 autodiff types.
9 
10 The first autodiff type in Stan was `var`. A `var` is the autodiff version
11 of a `double`. Constructing matrix autodiff types with `var` is as simple
12 as building an Eigen matrix and filling it with vars, so
13 `Eigen::Matrix<var, Eigen::Dynamic, Eigen::Dynamic>`. This is an AOS
14 autodiff style.
15 
16 The SOA autodiff style defines vector and matrices themselves as autodiff
17 variables. The SOA matrix is `var_value<Eigen::MatrixXd>`. Defining an
18 entire matrix as an autodiff type makes it possible to do a number of
19 optimizations that are not possible when the autodiff types are just
20 variables stored inside another structure.

24 ### Tests (SOA types)
25 
26 SOA tests are separate from the regular AOS tests. To test SOA types, add for
27 every `expect_ad` call an `expect_ad_matvar` call. `expect_ad` builds on the
28 `prim` tests by using finite derivatives to check all of the higher order
29 autodiff. `expect_ad_matvar` builds on the `expect_ad` tests by checking the
30 SOA autodiff agains the AOS autodiff.
